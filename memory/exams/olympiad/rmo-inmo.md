## IOQM — Indian Olympiad Qualifier in Mathematics (Stage 1)
exam_id: IOQM-{YEAR}
duration: 180 min | total_marks: 100 | neg: 0

| Type | Q | Marks each | Total |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1-digit answers (0–9) | 20 | 2 | 40 |
| 2-digit answers (00–99) | 10 | 3 | 30 |
| 2-digit answers (harder) | 6 | 5 | 30 |

Note: Numerical answer type only — no MCQ. Conducted by MTA (Mathematics Teachers Association).

## RMO — Regional Mathematical Olympiad (Stage 2)
exam_id: RMO-{YEAR}
duration: 180 min | ~6 subjective problems | Full proof-based solutions
Marks: ~6 problems × varies | Conducted region-wise

## INMO — Indian National Mathematical Olympiad (Stage 3)
exam_id: INMO-{YEAR}
duration: 240 min | 6 problems | Full proof-based
Conducted by HBCSE; top ~30–35 qualify for IMOTC

## IMOTC (Indian Mathematical Olympiad Training Camp)
Final selection camp; ~6 students chosen for IMO team

## Subject Codes
MATH

## QID Types
N (numerical) | D (descriptive/proof-based)

## Key Notes
- Classes 8–12 eligible (under 20 years on June 30 of exam year)
- No negative marking at any stage
- IOQM (formerly PRMO) is the entry point
- Proof-writing skills essential from RMO onwards
- IMO (International Mathematical Olympiad) — final goal, 6 members per country
- One of the most prestigious academic competitions in India
